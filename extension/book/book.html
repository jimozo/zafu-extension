How Zafu Works

Zafu watches every address you paste into crypto sites and checks it against your transaction history before it reaches the field. It runs entirely on your device — no keys, no signing, no external tracking.

Detection Layers

Every paste runs through 5 layers in order. First match wins.

  1. Clipboard guard — pasted address ≠ copied address → instant HIJACKED block. No network needed.
  2. Your history — trusted (you sent to) and suspicious (sent to you uninvited) addresses from your wallet transactions. Fully local.
  3. Curated blocklist — 10,000+ confirmed scam addresses bundled and refreshed weekly from ScamSniffer. No network needed.
  4. Community blocklist — crowd-sourced attacker addresses flagged by Zafu users. Fetched on startup, updated every 24h.
  5. GoPlus Security — real-time on-chain reputation API for EVM addresses. Last-resort check when all local layers return unknown.

Getting Started

  1. Open the Settings tab and paste your Etherscan API key (free at etherscan.io/apidashboard)
  2. Go to the Wallets tab and add your public wallet address
  3. Click Fetch — Zafu loads your full transaction history and builds your trusted address index
  4. That's it. From now on, Zafu runs automatically whenever you paste an address on a supported crypto site

Guardian Mode

Guardian Mode adds a paste preview step before every crypto-address paste. You'll see the address (and the resolved wallet address if you pasted an ENS name like vitalik.eth) before Zafu runs its full detection pipeline.

This catches ENS phishing — where a fake ENS name resolves to an attacker's wallet instead of the expected one. Enable Guardian Mode in the Settings tab.

Trusted vs. Suspicious

Trusted addresses are ones you actively sent ETH or tokens to. These get the green banner on paste and auto-confirm.

Suspicious addresses appeared in your transaction history but you never initiated a send to them — airdrops, dust attacks, spam token transfers, inbound payments from unknown sources. Zafu keeps these clearly separate. You can promote any suspicious address to trusted after giving it a label.

Supported Sites

Zafu intercepts pastes on Uniswap, 1inch, MetaMask, Rabby, Coinbase Wallet, Binance, Kraken, Aave, Compound, Etherscan, PolygonScan, and 30+ more crypto interfaces. On all other sites, it monitors input labels (recipient, send, withdraw) and intervenes if an address-shaped input is detected.

Permissions

Zafu requests 4 permissions. The average Chrome extension requests 17.

storage Saves your wallet list, address index, settings, and local community cache.
tabs Opens Zafu pages and clicked links in new tabs.
alarms Schedules 24h refreshes for opted-in wallets and community intelligence.
identity Optional Google Sign-In for address-book backup and restore.

No webRequest, no nativeMessaging, no clipboardRead. Zafu cannot sign transactions, move funds, or read private keys — by architecture, not policy.

Zero Telemetry

No analytics, no error reporting, no advertising tracking. Your labels, notes, and trusted contacts stay local unless you choose Google Sign-In for address-book backup. Community threat signals can share suspected attacker addresses and signal types to protect other users.

Public Source

The Chrome extension source is public and auditable. No bundler, no npm dependencies, no CDN scripts. What you read is what runs. The strict Content Security Policy enforces this at the browser level — no eval, no remote script injection possible.

How This Works

  1. Auto-detection — when Zafu indexes your wallet history, dust and zero-value inbound transactions are quietly submitted as community signals.
  2. One-click flagging — tap "Report as malicious" on any unknown address overlay. Multiple users flagging the same address promotes it to the blocklist.
  3. Threshold review — addresses crossing the signal threshold are analyzed on-chain and reviewed before being added.
  4. Sign in for backup — saved wallets, trusted contacts, labels, and notes follow your Google account.
