How Zafu Works

Zafu is recipient memory for stablecoin sends. From any wallet or exchange, it remembers who you pay and on which route, keeps risky history separate, and runs a final check before an address reaches a wallet, exchange, or dapp field.

Recipient Confidence States

Every trusted recipient carries one confidence state, computed from your local send history and what Zafu can check. It is the headline you read before sending.

StateWhat it means
Known recipientYou confirmed a send to this address on this device. The strongest state.
Checked routeSend history for this route was adopted from another device via account sync.
Needs testA usable stablecoin route, but no send confirmed here yet — send a small test first.
Saved instructionYou saved a partial route (asset, network, or memo) but it is not confirmed.
ReviewSomething needs attention: a network mismatch, a stale route, a duplicate, or a lookalike.
Network not checkedNo on-chain route has been checked for this contact yet.

A recipient moves up as you confirm sends (see Confirming routes) — for example Needs test → Known recipient the first time you mark a send.

Transfer Check

Every paste runs through five checks. First match wins.

  1. Clipboard guard — pasted address differs from the last same-chain browser copy → Possible Clipboard Mismatch review. No network needed.
  2. Your history — trusted (you sent to) and suspicious (sent to you uninvited) addresses from your wallet transactions. Fully local.
  3. Curated blocklist — 10,000+ confirmed scam addresses bundled and refreshed weekly from ScamSniffer. No network needed.
  4. Community warnings — crowd-sourced attacker addresses flagged by Zafu users. Fetched on startup, updated every 24h.
  5. GoPlus Security — real-time on-chain reputation API for EVM addresses. Last-resort check when all local layers return unknown.

After detection runs, Transfer Check shows observed, checked, warned, and not-checked evidence: asset and network hints when visible, copied-address match, source evidence, threat signals, and the full address before paste. When the address matches a saved recipient, it names them — for example "Sending to Maria — confirm you selected USDT on TRON/TRC-20." Tune the help level (Guided / Standard / Operator) or turn Transfer Check off in the Settings tab.

Address Intel & Review

Intel reviews a single address with your own explorer keys (Etherscan / Solscan / Tronscan) and reports a verdict badge: EOA (a normal wallet), Verified contract or Unverified contract, a known identity label (ENS or contract name), Balance checked, No risk found, or Risk flagged. The (i) icon explains the verdict and Details opens the full profile. Intel is local — your keys stay on this device and are never synced or sent to Zafu.

The sidebar Explorer Intel filter tracks each contact's review status so you can work through them: Needs review (Intel not run yet), Reviewed (run, clean), Risk flagged, or Review failed. The per-row Review button runs Intel for that one contact. This "review" is about address reputation — not the Review confidence state above, and not the import review below.

Building Recipient Memory

  1. Save a recipient manually, or import public wallet history in Wallets. Zafu cannot connect to a wallet or move funds.
  2. Imports are reviewed before saving — Zafu previews candidates ("Review all N individually") and never auto-trusts. TRON history imports open in Full View for the same review.
  3. Recipients you sent to become Trusted; unexpected inbound or dust addresses stay Suspicious (airdrops, dust attacks, spam transfers). Promote a suspicious address to trusted after labelling it.
  4. Each saved recipient keeps its provenance — whether it came from Telegram, a receive card, wallet history, or manual entry.

Confirming Routes: Receipts vs. Verification

Two complementary ways to raise a recipient's confidence:

  • Mark transfer sent — a self-attested receipt. You confirm a send you already made; Zafu records it on this device and flips Needs test → Known recipient. This records what you marked; it does not verify on-chain settlement.
  • Verify history — the table-header batch button re-checks saved contacts for real USDT/USDC on-chain history using your local explorer keys, confirming routes objectively. Contacts without a matching key stay saved.

Run Intel and Verify history sit side by side but do different jobs: Run Intel checks address reputation and identity (contract, labels, risk, balance); Verify history confirms stablecoin send history to set route confidence.

Receive & Handoff

Receive Instruction builds a safe "send me X on this network" message bound to one of your saved wallets, with the network restricted to that address's family — so you cannot offer an impossible asset/network combination. For outgoing handoffs, every row has Copy (with a safe-use reminder of the recipient's asset and route), a QR code, and Details for the full address.

Chains & Assets

Zafu supports EVM chains (Ethereum, Base, Polygon, Arbitrum, Optimism, BNB), TRON (USDT TRC-20, a major stablecoin rail), and Solana. Address format, segmentation, copy-vs-paste comparison, and Transfer Check work across all three. Explorer Intel uses the matching provider key for each chain.

Permissions

Zafu requests 3 permissions. The average Chrome extension requests 17.

storage Saves your wallet list, address index, settings, and local community cache.
alarms Schedules 24h refreshes for opted-in wallets and community intelligence.
identity Optional Google Sign-In for address-book backup and restore.

No webRequest, no nativeMessaging, no clipboardRead. Zafu cannot sign transactions, move funds, or read private keys — by architecture, not policy.

Network Mode

No advertising tracking or error reporting. Optional Network Mode shares anonymous aggregate counts only, never addresses, labels, clipboard text, chat text, URLs, balances, transaction hashes, Google ID, or email. Your labels, notes, and trusted contacts stay local unless you choose Google Sign-In for address-book backup. Community reports are separate from Network Mode and are controlled in the Community tab.

Public Source

The Chrome extension source is public and auditable. No bundler, no npm dependencies, no CDN scripts. What you read is what runs. The strict Content Security Policy enforces this at the browser level — no eval, no remote script injection possible.

Community Warnings
Zafu downloads community warning signals automatically and checks them during crypto-address pastes. This is a warning feed, not a browsable address directory.
— community warning feed

The feed is loaded locally and checked automatically on every crypto-address paste alongside Zafu's bundled risk data. You do not need to contribute reports to receive these warnings.

How This Works

  1. Warning feed — downloaded automatically and checked during Transfer Check.
  2. Manual reports — tap "Report as malicious" on an unknown address overlay to submit that suspected attacker address and signal type.
  3. Opt-in automatic contribution — when enabled, only suspected attacker-pattern addresses from wallet-history dust or trusted external confirmations may be submitted.
  4. Threshold review — community-only addresses must cross a signal threshold and stay explainable before warning other users.
  5. Sign in for backup — saved wallets, recipients, labels, and notes follow your Google account. Community warnings work without signing in.
